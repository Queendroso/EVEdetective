What Are Viral Fossils?

Endogenous Viral Elements (EVEs) are ancient viral DNA sequences that became part of a host’s genome. They’re like fossils written in our genetic code.

8%

8% of Human DNA

About 8% of the human genome consists of viral remnants from infections our ancestors survived millions of years ago.

Fruit Flies as Detectives

Drosophila melanogaster shares many genes with humans, revealing health insights through fly genetics.

Ancient Immunity

Glossary

Endogenous Viral Elements (EVEs)

Ancient viral DNA sequences that became part of a host’s genome — “viral fossils” written in DNA.

Retrovirus vs DNA virus

Retroviruses copy RNA → DNA and can integrate; DNA viruses carry DNA and usually don’t integrate.

piRNAs / piRNA clusters

Small RNAs and genomic regions that help silence transposons and viral sequences, especially in germ cells.

Outgroup

A distantly related species used to root a tree and identify the oldest split.

Intact / Useful / Broken / Unique

Intact/Useful EVEs may aid defense; Broken are degraded; Unique marks changes after two lineages split.

Common ancestry / phylogeny

Shared EVEs (same position/type) signal closer relationships among species.
